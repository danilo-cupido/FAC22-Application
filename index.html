My journey

I was born in Italy and lived most of my life in a beautiful village by the lake. I’ve always been a daydreamer and growing up I’ve become an idealist and activist. I got my bachelor degree in Development Studies with the hope to find my way in some Non-governmental organisations or Charities. Because of my total lack of knowledge of the English language I decided to move to London and stay for a short period of time in order to learn the language. Here I worked as a barista until I lost my job due to the recent pandemic. During this year I’ve learnt how valuable is to live in a multicultural society and I made some very good friends from every corner of the globe. And I’ve found the love of my life!

Coding

My first contact with coding was in the middle of the eighties when I was a child and me and my brother used to write some programmes in Basic and run it on an MSX. During the high school I had the opportunity to learn Turbo Pascal but by the time of the Uni I was lost in Politics and Social Activism so I put my passion for coding aside until a few months ago. Talking with my wife, who is a developer, I told her of the passion I once had for coding and that now I regret that I didn’t follow that path. She invited me to watch some video tutorials and try freeCodeCamp. And now here we are! Coding is my passion again and I would love to become a developer on day!
